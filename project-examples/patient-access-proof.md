# Patient Access Proof

## Purpose

This proof example shows how patient access workflows affect scheduling, eligibility, authorization readiness, financial clearance, communication, and downstream revenue cycle outcomes.

## Core Insight

Patient access is the front door of revenue cycle performance. When intake, scheduling, eligibility, authorization, and financial clearance are unstable, the downstream impact often appears later as denials, delays, rework, A/R aging, and patient confusion.

## Workflow Map

```text
Appointment Requested
  |
  v
Patient Information Collected
  |
  v
Insurance Verified
  |
  v
Payer Rules Checked
  |
  v
Authorization or Referral Reviewed
  |
  v
Financial Responsibility Communicated
  |
  v
Account Cleared Before Service
```

## Common Failure Points

| Failure Point | Downstream Risk | Prevention Action |
|---|---|---|
| Incomplete intake data | Registration rework or claim rejection | Standardize intake fields |
| Insurance not verified | Eligibility denial | Confirm active coverage before service |
| Authorization need missed | Authorization denial | Check payer rules before appointment |
| Patient responsibility unclear | Billing confusion | Communicate estimate before service |
| Clearance status not visible | Scheduling and billing delays | Use account clearance status tracking |

## Metric to Track

Financial clearance completion rate, eligibility-related denial rate, authorization-related denial rate, patient access delay volume, and registration error rate.
