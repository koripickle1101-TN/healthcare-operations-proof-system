## Purpose

This proof-of-work case study shows how prior authorization functions as a coordination workflow across patient access, payer rules, clinical documentation, status tracking, approval verification, and final account clearance.

The goal is to demonstrate how authorization breakdowns can create delayed care, preventable denials, reimbursement delays, A/R rework, and patient frustration.

## Core Analyst Insight

Prior authorization is not just a payer requirement.

It is a workflow visibility problem when ownership, documentation, payer rules, timing, and status tracking are unclear.

## Problem

Prior authorization failures often begin before the claim is ever submitted. When payer requirements are not checked early, documentation is incomplete, or pending authorization status is not tracked before the service date, the downstream impact may appear later as a denial, delayed reimbursement, last-minute rescheduling, or patient billing confusion.

Common authorization breakdowns include:

- authorization requirement not checked before appointment confirmation
- wrong payer or plan used for the authorization request
- missing clinical documentation
- payer request submitted with incomplete details
- pending authorization status not tracked before service
- approval number or valid date range not documented
- authorization status not handed off clearly to scheduling, billing, or clinical teams

## Root-Cause Breakdown

| Authorization Breakdown | Where It Starts | Downstream Risk | Prevention Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Requirement not checked | Scheduling or patient access | Missing authorization denial | Check payer rules before appointment confirmation |
| Wrong payer or plan selected | Registration or payer setup | Authorization submitted to wrong payer | Validate payer, plan type, and coverage record before submission |
| Documentation incomplete | Clinical documentation | Authorization delay or denial | Use documentation readiness checklist before submission |
| Incomplete authorization request | Authorization submission | Payer rejection or delayed review | Standardize required authorization fields |
| Pending status not tracked | Authorization follow-up | Appointment may occur before approval | Use authorization workqueue by service date and urgency |
| Approval details missing | Final clearance or billing | Claim denial or billing rework | Verify approval number, valid dates, approved service, and provider details |
| Handoff unclear | Cross-department workflow | Duplicate work, missed follow-up, or unclear patient communication | Create standardized authorization status handoff note |

## Operational Impact

When prior authorization workflow visibility is weak, the organization may experience:

- delayed patient care
- last-minute appointment cancellations
- missing authorization denials
- delayed reimbursement
- increased A/R workload
- staff rework
- patient frustration
- unclear ownership across departments
- weaker trust in scheduling and financial clearance workflows

## Prevention Strategy

A stronger prior authorization workflow should make the requirement, owner, status, next action, and deadline visible before the service occurs.

Recommended checkpoints:

- confirm payer and plan before authorization review
- identify whether the service requires authorization
- confirm service, procedure, provider, and diagnosis details
- collect required clinical documentation
- submit authorization with complete payer-required information
- track pending status by service date
- verify approval number and valid date range
- document authorization status in the account
- clear the account before the appointment occurs
- hand off unresolved authorization issues to a named owner

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| Authorization-related denial rate | Whether payer-rule checks are happening before service |
| Average authorization turnaround time | Whether approvals are being obtained in time for scheduled care |
| Pending authorizations by service date | Whether upcoming appointments are at risk |
| Authorization rework volume | Whether submissions are incomplete or inaccurate |
| Missing approval number cases | Whether approved authorizations are being documented correctly |
| Invalid date range denial volume | Whether approval validity is being verified before service |
| Patient access delay volume | Whether authorization issues are delaying scheduled care |

## Analyst Recommendation

Prior authorization should be treated as a coordination workflow, not just a payer task. The strongest improvement opportunity is to create a pre-service authorization control point that connects payer rules, documentation readiness, status tracking, account clearance, and handoff ownership before the date of service.
